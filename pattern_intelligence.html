The 6 Patterns

Every NEET question
is one of these.

Examiners have been recycling the same 6 structures for decades. Once you can name the pattern, you know exactly which mental move to make before reading a single option.

🎯
Memory Test
Direct Recall
Fact from NCERT, stated directly. No inversion, no trick. The only skill here is whether you read that page. These are the fastest 4 marks available — never leave one blank.
Pure recall
⚡
Negative Charge
NOT / EXCEPT / Incorrect
The question flips. "Which is NOT true?" Three options are correct NCERT facts — which makes them feel like the answer. Your brain is primed to find what's right. Slow down. Reread the stem every time.
Inversion trap
🌀
Concept Guru
Multi-Statement Evaluation
Statements a, b, c are given. Students verify two, feel confident, and miss the one buried falsehood. Every statement must be independently verified — there's no safe shortcut here.
Statement combo
🖼️
Diagram Dhamaka
Visual / Labelling
A diagram, table, or chart requires interpretation. Students who only read text get trapped. The content may be familiar but the visual format changes what you need to retrieve.
Visual reading
⛔
Speed Breaker
Order / Sequence / Match
Correct sequence, ascending/descending order, match the column. Looks doable but eats time. Students spend 3× longer here than on Memory Tests. Anchor on one certain pair, eliminate half the options, move on.
Time trap
🔽
Best Choice
Elimination Required
No single option is obviously right. Two or three sound plausible. The correct approach is to eliminate clearly wrong answers first. The correct answer often survives, not wins.
Process of elimination

🎯 Memory Test Biology · Plant Kingdom

Which pigment is responsible for the characteristic colour of red algae?

AFucoxanthin
Br-Phycoerythrin
CChlorophyll b
DCarotenoids
The move: Pure recall. No trick. You know it or you don't. NCERT states r-phycoerythrin gives red algae their colour. This is worth 4 marks — just remember it.
✓ Answer: B
⚡ Negative Charge Biology · Cell: The Unit of Life · NEET 2019

Which of the following is NOT true about ribosomes?

AThey are the site of protein synthesis
BThey are made of RNA and protein
C80S ribosomes are found in prokaryotes
D70S ribosomes are found in chloroplasts
The trap: Your brain is primed to find what IS true. Options A, B, D are all correct NCERT facts — they feel like the answer. The "NOT" is doing all the work. Reread the stem before touching the options.
✓ Answer: C
🌀 Concept Guru Biology · Principles of Inheritance · NEET 2018

Identify the incorrect statement about repetitive sequences:

(a) These sequences are of no transcriptional function
(b) These are associated with euchromatin region
(c) These help identify a person on the basis of DNA specificity

AAll are correct
BOnly (b) is incorrect
CBoth (a) & (b) are correct
DBoth (b) & (c) are incorrect
The trap: Students verify (a) and (c) — both correct — then select "All correct." But (b) is wrong: repetitive sequences are in heterochromatin, not euchromatin. One buried error invalidates the whole set. Verify every statement independently.
✓ Answer: B
🔽 Best Choice Physics · Electromagnetic Induction

An emf can be induced in a stationary coil if it is kept in a:

AStationary uniform magnetic field
BStationary non-uniform magnetic field
CTime-varying magnetic field
DStrong magnetic field
The move: A and B are static fields — no change, no induction. D sounds right intuitively. Faraday's law is explicit: rate of change of flux induces emf. Eliminate A, B, D. Only C survives. Don't solve — shortlist.
✓ Answer: C
⛔ Speed Breaker Chemistry · Coordination Compounds · NEET 2020

Match the ion with its analytical group:

A. Co²⁺    B. Mg²⁺    C. Pb²⁺    D. Al³⁺

AA-II, B-VI, C-IV, D-III
BA-III, B-VI, C-II, D-IV
CA-VI, B-III, C-II, D-IV
DA-IV, B-III, C-VI, D-II
The move: Anchor on one certain pair. If you're sure Al³⁺ is Group IV, only B and C remain — you've halved the problem in 5 seconds. Never match all four at once. One anchor, two options, done.
✓ Answer: C
The Engine

How every question
gets classified

Pattern tagging is built into every question in the NEETminds database. Every one of the 5,076 questions carries a pattern code — so the practice filter shows exactly which pattern you're weak on.

01
Question ingested
Every question from NEET papers and NCERT source material enters the pipeline with its original text preserved verbatim.
02
Trigger detection
Structural signals are scanned: negation words (NOT, EXCEPT, incorrect), statement blocks (a)(b)(c), match columns, order requests, assertion markers.
03
Pattern assigned
One of 6 pattern codes is attached. If multiple triggers fire, the dominant trap type wins. Every question has exactly one pattern label.
04
Difficulty calibrated
Negative Charge and Concept Guru auto-escalate in difficulty. Memory Test stays efficient. Your practice gets harder as pattern fluency improves.
