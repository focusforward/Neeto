The Pattern Library

The 6 Patterns Behind Every NEET Question

Every question in NEET fits one of these six patterns. Spot the pattern first — then apply the right strategy. That's the NEETMINDS™ system.

01
🎯
~45% of NEET
Memory Test
"Straight from NCERT" — the most common pattern. Question asks exactly what you've read. No traps, no flips. Just recall.
"Respiratory quotient for fatty acid is:" — you either remember it's <1 or you don't.
Strategy: Pure recall. If you've read NCERT, you get it right. No elimination needed. Master this pattern first — it's almost half the paper.
02
🔽
~14% of NEET
Best Choice
"Most appropriate / most likely / best describes" — no single option is obviously right. Two options will be close. This is an elimination tournament, not a memory test.
"Which of the following best describes the role of oxytocin in parturition?" — two will be true, one is most complete.
Strategy: Eliminate the two clearly wrong options first. Then compare the final two on a single axis: which is more complete, more accurate, or more directly answering the question stem?
03
🌀
~9% of NEET
Concept Guru
Assertion-Reason questions where the Reason is factually correct — but doesn't actually explain the Assertion. Students who don't check the logical link fall every time.
Assertion: Both statements are correct. Reason: The reason correctly explains the assertion.
Strategy: Check the logical link, not just truth value. Both A and R can be true — but if R doesn't explain A, the answer is "Both true but R NOT correct explanation."
04
⚡
~10% of NEET
Negative Charge
"Which of the following is NOT correct / EXCEPT / INCORRECT?" — the question flips your entire mental model. Students lose marks here by answering the right question to the wrong question.
"All of the following are true about meiosis EXCEPT:" — your job is to find the false one.
Strategy: Underline NOT/EXCEPT/INCORRECT the moment you see it. Read options as "true or false" statements. Your job is to find the one that's FALSE — not what's correct.
05
⛔
~8% of NEET
Speed Breaker
Match-the-column, correct-sequence, and correct-order questions. Designed to eat 3+ minutes of your time if you approach them without a system.
"Match Column I with Column II and select the correct option:"
Strategy: Start with the pairing you're most confident about. Eliminate options where that pairing is wrong. Often leaves 1–2 options. One more check closes it. Never verify all pairs.
06
🖼️
~4% of NEET
Diagram Dhamaka
Figure-based questions — label the diagram, identify the structure, interpret the graph. Your NCERT diagrams are your answer key here.
"Identify the structure marked X in the given diagram of nephron:"
Strategy: When studying, don't just read diagrams — draw them. Label them from memory. The 4% it represents is fully claimable if you've actively practised visual recall over passive reading.
The Training System

How NEETMINDS™ Trains Pattern Intelligence

1

Identify the Pattern Before Reading Options

Every question in NEETMINDS™ is tagged to one of the 6 patterns. Before you answer, you learn to ask: Is this a Memory Test? A Negative Charge? A Concept Guru? Pattern recognition switches the cognitive load from memory to strategy.

2

Answer with the Pattern's Decision Rule

Each pattern has a specific decision process. Negative Charge? Read every option for the false statement. Concept Guru? Check the logical link — not just the truth value. You stop guessing. You start executing a system.

3

Explanation Trains the Examiner's Logic

After every answer, the explanation doesn't just tell you what's right. It shows you why the examiner designed it that way — what trap was set, who it catches, and how to sidestep it permanently.

4

Mock Tests Reinforce Under Exam Pressure

Full 200-question, 200-minute mocks simulate real NEET conditions. Pattern recognition is useless if it breaks under pressure. Mock tests build the automatic, calm pattern-identification that turns trained intelligence into actual marks.
