The Pattern Library

The 6 Patterns Behind Every NEET Question

Every question you've ever seen in NEET fits into one of these six categories. Know the pattern before you read the options.

01
🎭
~45% of NEET
The Exception Trap
"Which of the following is NOT correct?" The examiner lists 3 true statements and 1 subtly false one. The trap is reading too fast and confirming the truths without checking the exception.
"All of the following are correct about mitosis EXCEPT…" — Read every option with suspicion.
02
🔗
~20% of NEET
The Cause-Gets-Garbled
An Assertion-Reason question where the Reason is factually correct — but doesn't actually explain the Assertion. Students who don't check the logical link fall for it every time.
A is true, R is true, but R does not explain A. Answer is immediately (A) without second-guessing.
03
🧩
~14% of NEET
The Boundary Confusion
Questions that test whether you know the exact boundary of a concept — where one definition ends and another begins. These punish vague understanding.
"Which of the following is a gymnosperm but NOT a conifer?" — the boundary between overlapping categories.
04
🔢
~10% of NEET
The Number Confusion
Specific numbers — chromosomes, stages, days, equations — are swapped or slightly altered. One digit off and you lose a mark. These are pure precision tests.
"The number of chromosomes in a human secondary oocyte at metaphase II is…" — exact numbers only.
05
🚦
~8% of NEET
The Speed Breaker
Long, intimidating question stems designed to slow you down. The actual content being tested is simple — but the framing creates cognitive overload.
Three-line scenario about a patient, a diagram, and a lab result — asking something you learned in Class 11.
06
🔁
~4% of NEET
The Definition Drift
NCERT uses a specific word in a specific context. The examiner rephrases it slightly. Students who paraphrased during revision select the "almost correct" answer.
"The process by which…" — NCERT's exact wording matters more than your paraphrase.
The Training System

How NEETMINDS™ Trains Pattern Intelligence

1

Identify the Pattern Before Reading Options

Every question in NEETMINDS™ is tagged to one of the 6 patterns. Before you answer, you learn to ask: Is this an Exception Trap? A Speed Breaker? A Boundary Confusion? Pattern recognition switches the cognitive load from memory to strategy.

2

Answer with the Pattern's Decision Rule

Each pattern has a specific decision process. Exception Trap? Read every option for the false statement. Cause-Gets-Garbled? Check the logical link, not just the truth value. You stop guessing. You start executing a system.

3

Explanation Trains the Examiner's Logic

After every answer, the explanation doesn't just tell you what's right. It shows you why the examiner designed it that way — what trap was set, who it catches, and how to sidestep it permanently.

4

Mock Tests Reinforce Under Exam Pressure

Full 200-question, 200-minute mocks simulate real NEET conditions. Pattern recognition is useless if it breaks under pressure. Mock tests build the automatic, calm pattern-identification that turns trained intelligence into actual marks.
