Memory Test
~45% of NEET · Most common

Direct factual recall questions. The examiner tests whether you've read NCERT. These are your fastest marks — one glance, one answer, move on.

Strategy: If you know it, mark it in under 20 seconds. If you genuinely don't recall it after 10 seconds, skip it. Never burn 90 seconds on a Memory Test you haven't studied.
NEET Weightage45%
Sample Memory Test question
Which of the following is NOT a characteristic feature of phylum Annelida?
A. Metamerism
B. Closed circulatory system
C. Triploblastic
D. Pseudocoelom
💡 Annelida have TRUE coelom, not pseudocoelom. Direct NCERT recall — one glance, done.
🌀
Concept Guru
~20% of NEET · Logic-first

Assertion-Reason questions and multi-statement (A, B, C, D — which are correct?) formats. These test logical connections, not isolated memory.

Strategy: Evaluate each statement independently. The trap is assuming statements are connected — they may not be. Check the Reason's causal link to the Assertion separately from whether both are true.
NEET Weightage20%
Sample Concept Guru question
Assertion (A): All enzymes are proteins. Reason (R): Enzymes have specific three-dimensional structure essential for catalysis.
A. Both A and R true; R is correct explanation
B. Both A and R true; R is NOT correct explanation
C. A is true, R is false
D. A is false, R is true
💡 Ribozymes (RNA) are enzymes — so Assertion is false. R is independently true. Evaluate each statement alone.
🔽
Best Choice
~14% of NEET · Elimination game

"Most appropriate / most likely / best describes" — no single option is obviously right. Two options will be close. This is an elimination tournament, not a memory test.

Strategy: Eliminate the two clearly wrong options first. Then compare the final two on a single axis: which is more complete, more accurate, or more directly answering the question stem?
NEET Weightage14%
Sample Best Choice question
Which of the following BEST describes the role of ATP in cellular reactions?
A. Acts as a coenzyme in all reactions
B. Stores genetic information
C. Serves as the primary energy currency
D. Catalyses metabolic reactions
💡 Eliminate B (DNA) and D (enzymes do this). Between A and C — ATP isn't a coenzyme in ALL reactions. C is more complete and accurate.
⚡
Negative Charge
~10% of NEET · Mental flip required

"Which of the following is NOT correct / EXCEPT / INCORRECT?" — the question flips your entire mental model. Students lose marks here by answering the right question to the wrong question.

Strategy: Underline NOT/EXCEPT/INCORRECT the moment you see it. Read options as "true or false" statements. Your job is to find the one that's FALSE — not to find what's correct.
NEET Weightage10%
Sample Negative Charge question
Which of the following is NOT a function of the liver?
A. Synthesis of urea
B. Glycogen storage
C. Production of insulin
D. Bile secretion
💡 Underline NOT first. A, B, D are all liver functions. Insulin is made by pancreatic β-cells, not liver.
⛔
Speed Breaker
~8% of NEET · Time trap

Match-the-column, correct-sequence, and correct-order questions. Designed to eat 3+ minutes of your time if you approach them without a system.

Strategy: Start with the pairing you're most confident about. Eliminate options where your confident pairing is wrong. Often this leaves 1–2 options. One more confident check closes it. Never attempt to verify all pairs.
NEET Weightage8%
Sample Speed Breaker question
Match List I (Ecological pyramid) with List II (characteristic).
A. A-III, B-I, C-IV, D-II
B. A-II, B-IV, C-I, D-III
C. A-I, B-II, C-III, D-IV
D. A-IV, B-III, C-II, D-I
💡 Start with the pairing you're most confident about. Lock it. Eliminate 2–3 options. One more check closes it.
🖼️
Diagram Dhamaka
~4% of NEET · Visual recall

Figure-based questions — label the diagram, identify the structure, interpret the graph. Your NCERT diagrams are your answer key here.

Strategy: When studying, don't just read diagrams — draw them. Label them from memory. The 4% it represents in NEET is fully claimable if you've actively practised visual recall rather than passive reading.
NEET Weightage4%
Sample Diagram Dhamaka question
In the diagram of a nephron, the structure labelled 'X' represents:
A. Bowman's capsule
B. Loop of Henle
C. Proximal convoluted tubule ✓
D. Collecting duct
💡 Know your NCERT diagrams cold. The PCT is the first convoluted section after Bowman's capsule — drawn from memory, this is instant.

The 3-Step System
for Every Question

01

Name the Pattern

Before reading options, identify which of the 6 patterns this is. The question stem gives it away — you'll start seeing it within 3 seconds once trained.

02

Apply the Strategy

Each pattern has one correct attack strategy. Don't think — execute. The strategy is pre-loaded. This is what saves you 90 seconds per question over 200 questions.

03

Mark and Move

Commit to your answer in the allowed time. Overthinking after pattern recognition is what kills scores. Trust the system. Review only if time remains at the end.
